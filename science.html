Michigan Science Standards (MSS). Adopted Nov. 10, 2015 — Michigan adopted the Next Generation Science Standards (NGSS) verbatim. Standards are organized by Disciplinary Core Ideas (DCIs), Cross-Cutting Concepts, and Science & Engineering Practices. NWEA MAP Growth does NOT cover HS Biology or Chemistry — these subjects are outside the MAP test family.

About the Michigan Science Standards

Michigan's Science Standards mirror NGSS for K–12. HS Biology and Chemistry are anchored in Disciplinary Core Ideas with codes like HS-LS1-1 (Life Sciences) and HS-PS1-1 (Physical Sciences). Each standard integrates content with science practices (asking questions, analyzing data, constructing explanations, etc.) and cross-cutting concepts (cause/effect, systems, energy, structure/function).

College Board offers seven science AP exams. Troy HS confirmed offerings (per Wikipedia + course catalog) include AP Biology, AP Chemistry, AP Environmental Science, and AP Physics 1 (Athens HS lists additional courses).

Troy SD test-out for Biology and Chemistry

Important: Troy's non-math test-out policy is materially different from math. The district's non-math test-out page explicitly states: "Students will not be provided with study guides, study materials, or course syllabi. However, students can review Michigan state standards for courses for which they wish to attempt to test out."
  • All required courses are eligible (Biology and Chemistry are required for HS graduation in MI). Electives are not eligible.
  • Passing threshold: 77%
  • Successful test-out earns "G" grade (credit, no letter grade) on transcript. Failed attempts are not recorded.
  • Form: "Waiving out of a NON-MATH course through Testing Out" in PowerSchool
  • Study path: Review Michigan Science Standards (HS-LS1 through HS-LS4 for Biology; HS-PS1, HS-PS2, HS-PS3 for Chemistry) — see standards links above
  • Note: 7th graders cannot test out of Introductory Biology because Chemistry 1 / Physics 1 are not available to 8th graders
