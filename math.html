RIT bands are typical, not prescriptive. They reflect where on-grade-level students working at these domains commonly score in NWEA's Learning Continuum. Individual student bands vary. Course-Specific MAP Growth tests (Algebra 1, Geometry, Algebra 2) are administered during the course, not as placement screens.

NWEA MAP Growth Math · test families at a glance

Different MAP Growth Math tests cover different grade bands. A 5th grader is on the 2-5 form; a 6th grader moves to 6+. The instructional-area labels shift slightly between forms — here's the reference.

Throughlines · single concepts that thread K → 12

Seven concepts that evolve continuously from kindergarten through AP-level mathematics, and consistently land in a particular NWEA instructional area at each stage (where MAP coverage exists).

1. OAT → Expressions & Equations → Algebra

K–5: Operations & Algebraic Thinking domain
6–7: Expressions & Equations (6.EE, 7.EE)
8: Expressions & Equations + Functions (8.EE, 8.F)
HS: Algebra (A-SSE, A-APR, A-CED, A-REI) and Functions (F-IF, F-BF, F-LE)
AP: Pre-Calc / AP Precalc deepens function families; AP Calc applies algebra to limits and derivatives
NWEA: "OAT" → "OAT / Expressions & Equations" (6+) → "Algebra" + "Functions" (Course-Specific)
The spine of K–12 algebra readiness. Growth in OAT can be tracked from kindergarten through MAP's ceiling, then handed off to AP coursework.

2. Number Sense → Real Numbers → Complex Numbers → Limits

K–2: Counting and Cardinality + NBT
3–5: NBT + Fractions
6–7: The Number System (rationals, integers)
8: Number System (irrationals)
HS: N-RN, N-CN (complex), N-VM (vectors)
AP Calc: The number line becomes the foundation for limits, continuity, and convergence of series
NWEA: "Number & Operations" → "Real & Complex Number System" → "Number & Quantity"
Most stable longitudinal signal across all MAP tests; ends at MAP ceiling, becomes the substrate for AP Calc.

3. Geometry (spatial reasoning throughout)

K–5: Geometry domain (shapes, attributes, coordinate plane)
6–7: Area, surface area, volume; scale; circles
8: Transformations, Pythagorean, congruence/similarity
HS: G-CO, G-SRT, G-C, G-GPE, G-GMD, G-MG
NWEA: "Geometry" instructional area in every test family
The most stable label across all NWEA tests — a clean longitudinal measurement.

4. Measurement & Data → Statistics & Probability → Inference

K–5: Measurement & Data domain
6–8: Statistics & Probability emerges as its own domain
HS: S-ID, S-IC, S-CP, S-MD
AP Statistics: Sampling distributions, confidence intervals, hypothesis testing (z, t, χ²), inference for slope
NWEA: "Measurement & Data" (K-2, 2-5) → "Statistics & Probability" (6+, Course-Specific)
The transition involves a name change in NWEA's sub-scores around grade 5–6; AP Statistics moves from descriptive to inferential reasoning.

5. Ratios → Linear Functions → Function Families → Calculus

3–5: Multiplicative reasoning
6–7: Ratios & Proportional Relationships
8: Functions, especially linear
HS: F-IF, F-BF, F-LE; A-CED (modeling)
Pre-Calc: Polynomial, rational, exponential, log, trigonometric, polar function families
AP Calc: Functions become the object of differentiation and integration
NWEA: Distributed in 2-5 (OAT and N&O) → "OAT / Expressions & Equations" → "Functions"
The function concept's continuous evolution — from ratio table to function notation to derivative and integral.

6. Algebraic Modeling & Problem Solving (★)

K–5: Word problems embedded in OA, MD
6–8: Real-world problems in EE, RP, G, SP
HS: Modeling category (★ on standards across categories); A-CED specifically
AP Calc / AP Stats: Differential equations, accumulation, regression, study design as modeling
NWEA: Distributed across all instructional areas — not its own sub-score
Modeling growth has to be inferred from items spread across other sub-scores. Watch this gap.

7. Rate of Change → Slope → Derivative

3–5: Multiplicative comparison, unit rate
6–7: Constant of proportionality (k in y = kx)
8: Slope from similar triangles; y = mx + b
HS Algebra/Pre-Calc: Average rate of change of any function (F-IF.B.6); instantaneous rate
AP Calc: Derivative as the limit of the average rate of change; differential equations
NWEA: Distributed in OAT/RCNS; not its own sub-score above grade 8
Single most consequential mathematical idea — the entire K-12 path can be read as preparation for the derivative concept.
