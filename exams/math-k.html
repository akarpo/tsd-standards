Kindergarten Mathematics — Testing-Out Examination

A student who passes this examination has demonstrated mastery of the Common Core Kindergarten standards (K.CC, K.OA, K.NBT, K.MD, K.G) and is eligible to advance directly to Grade 1 Mathematics.

Format and proctor instructions

  • This examination is proctor-administered. The student is a kindergartner (5–6 years old). The proctor reads each task aloud and writes down what the student says on this answer sheet. The student uses real objects (counters, pattern blocks, shape cards) for most tasks.
  • Time: 120 minutes total in three sessions of about 30 minutes, separated by two 10-minute breaks. Sessions: Tasks 1–3, Tasks 4–6, Tasks 7–8.
  • Materials the proctor provides: a set of 30 same-color counters; a set of pattern-block shapes (triangle, square, rectangle, hexagon); 3-D shape models (cube, cone, cylinder, sphere); a printed picture sheet (proctor's set); pencil; crayons.
  • Scoring: Each task is worth the points shown. Award full credit only for fully correct, smooth answers; partial credit (half points, listed by sub-task) for answers that are mostly correct or that need one short proctor prompt.
  • Recording: The proctor writes the student's spoken answers in the space provided. For drawing or counting tasks, the student marks directly on the answer sheet.

SESSION 2 · Tasks 4–6 (about 30 minutes)

4. Make and break apart teen numbers 12 points
  1. Show 14 using one ten-rod and four ones (base-ten blocks). Then say: "14 is one 10 and 4 ones." Repeat for 11, 17, 19.

    Student demonstrations: 11 = ___ ten and ___ ones    17 = ___ ten and ___ ones    19 = ___ ten and ___ ones     Points: ___ / 6

  2. Break each teen number apart as \(10 + ?\):

    (i) \(13 = 10 + ?\)     (ii) \(15 = 10 + ?\)     (iii) \(18 = 10 + ?\)     Points: ___ / 3

  3. Build the number 16 in two different ways using counters and 10-frames. Sketch or describe each.

    Way 1: ____________________    Way 2: ____________________     Points: ___ / 3

5. Measurement attributes and comparison 12 points
  1. The proctor places three pencils of obviously different lengths on the table. The student orders them from shortest to longest.

    Order recorded by proctor: ____________________     Points: ___ / 2

  2. The proctor places two objects (e.g., a book and a stuffed animal) on the table. The student says which is heavier after lifting both.

    Student says: heavier = ____________________    lighter = ____________________     Points: ___ / 2

  3. The proctor sets out a mixed group of buttons: 4 red, 3 blue, 5 yellow. The student sorts them into groups by color and counts each.

    Counts: red ___, blue ___, yellow ___     Which group has the most? ___     Which has the fewest? ___     Points: ___ / 4

  4. The student arranges the three groups in order from fewest to most.

    Order: ____________________     Points: ___ / 2

  5. Describe one measurable attribute (length, weight, height) of a typical object, e.g., "This pencil is __________ (long / short / heavy / light)."

    Student response: ____________________     Points: ___ / 2

6. Name flat (2-D) shapes and position words 12 points
  1. The proctor shows the shape cards in turn: triangle, circle, square, rectangle, hexagon. The student names each and tells how many sides and corners (vertices).

    Triangle: ___ sides, ___ corners    Circle: ___ sides, ___ corners    Square: ___ sides, ___ corners    Rectangle: ___ sides, ___ corners    Hexagon: ___ sides, ___ corners     Points: ___ / 5

  2. The proctor places three objects on the table — a book, a cup, and a pencil. The proctor asks the student to describe positions:

    "Is the pencil above or below the book?" → ___
    "Is the cup beside or behind the book?" → ___
    "Is the pencil next to or far from the cup?" → ___     Points: ___ / 3

  3. Sketch each shape on the answer sheet:

    (i) a triangle     (ii) a square     (iii) a hexagon

  4. The proctor places two pattern-block triangles next to each other to form a larger shape. "What new shape did we make?" (Expected answer: a rhombus or "diamond" or larger triangle, depending on orientation.)

    Student response: ____________________     Points: ___ / 2
