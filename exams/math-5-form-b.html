Instructions

1. 5.OA · Order of operations & patterns 10 points
  1. Find the value using the order of operations. Show every step:

    (i) \(8 + 3 \times (12 - 5)\)    (ii) \( (24 - 6) \div 3 + 2 \)    (iii) \( 2 \times [3 + (10 - 4)] \)    (iv) \( 50 - 6 \times 4 + 18 \div 2 \)    (v) \( 100 \div (5 + 5) - 2 \)

  2. Write a numerical expression for each description in words (do not solve it):

    (i) "5 more than the product of 3 and 4"    (ii) "8 less than twice the sum of 4 and 7"    (iii) "the difference of 50 and 12, divided by 2"

  3. Two patterns. Pattern A: starts at 0, rule "add 4." Pattern B: starts at 0, rule "add 8." List the first six terms of each. Form ordered pairs \((A, B)\) using corresponding terms. State in one sentence the relationship between corresponding terms.
2. 5.NBT.A · Decimal place value & powers of 10 10 points
  1. State the value of the digit 7 in each:

    (i) 47.236    (ii) 0.0875    (iii) 7{,}142    (iv) 132.470

  2. Find each (no calculator):

    (i) \(4.36 \times 10\)    (ii) \(4.36 \times 100\)    (iii) \(4.36 \times 1000\)    (iv) \(4.36 \div 10\)    (v) \(4.36 \div 100\)    (vi) \(4.36 \div 1000\)

  3. Round each to the nearest tenth, hundredth, and thousandth:

    (i) 27.4856    (ii) 0.31572    (iii) 5.99499

  4. Compare each pair using \(<,\ >,\ =\):

    (i) 0.5 ___ 0.50    (ii) 0.4 ___ 0.39    (iii) 0.072 ___ 0.0720    (iv) 7.4 ___ 7.39

4. 5.NF.A · Add and subtract fractions (unlike denominators) 10 points
  1. Find each. Find a common denominator first; write in simplest form:

    (i) \( \dfrac{2}{3} + \dfrac{1}{4} \)    (ii) \( \dfrac{5}{6} - \dfrac{1}{4} \)    (iii) \( \dfrac{7}{8} + \dfrac{2}{3} \)    (iv) \( \dfrac{3}{5} - \dfrac{1}{10} \)

  2. Mixed numbers (regroup if needed):

    (i) \(2\dfrac{1}{2} + 1\dfrac{3}{8}\)    (ii) \(4\dfrac{1}{6} - 1\dfrac{2}{3}\)    (iii) \(5 - 2\dfrac{3}{4}\)    (iv) \(3\dfrac{2}{5} + 4\dfrac{3}{10}\)

5. 5.NF.B · Multiply and divide fractions 12 points
  1. Multiply (write in simplest form):

    (i) \( \dfrac{3}{5} \cdot \dfrac{10}{9} \)    (ii) \( \dfrac{2}{3} \cdot 12 \)    (iii) \( \dfrac{4}{7} \cdot \dfrac{14}{15} \)    (iv) \( 1\dfrac{1}{2} \cdot 2\dfrac{2}{3} \)

  2. Divide (reciprocal method):

    (i) \( \dfrac{3}{4} \div \dfrac{1}{2} \)    (ii) \( 6 \div \dfrac{2}{3} \)    (iii) \( \dfrac{5}{6} \div 4 \)    (iv) \( 2\dfrac{1}{4} \div \dfrac{3}{8} \)

  3. Word problems (write a number sentence first):

    (i) A rectangle is \( \dfrac{3}{4} \) m long and \( \dfrac{2}{3} \) m wide. Find its area.
    (ii) How many \( \dfrac{1}{6} \)-cup servings are in a \(2\dfrac{1}{2}\)-cup container?
    (iii) A baker uses \( \dfrac{2}{5} \) of a 3-lb bag of flour. How many lb did the baker use?

6. 5.MD.A · Measurement conversions 8 points
  1. Convert (show the conversion factor):

    (i) 5.2 km = ___ m    (ii) 4500 g = ___ kg    (iii) 3 hr 15 min = ___ min    (iv) 250 cm = ___ m    (v) 7 ft = ___ in    (vi) 8 cups = ___ qts

  2. Word problem: a recipe needs 2 L of water. The pitcher holds 750 mL. How many pitchers must be filled to get the 2 L? Round up.
  3. Word problem: a runner runs 4 laps around a 400-m track. Write the distance in km.
7. 5.MD.B · Line plots with fractions 5 points

Lengths of insects (in inches): \( \dfrac{1}{4},\ \dfrac{3}{8},\ \dfrac{1}{2},\ \dfrac{1}{2},\ \dfrac{3}{8},\ \dfrac{5}{8},\ \dfrac{3}{4},\ \dfrac{1}{2},\ \dfrac{3}{8},\ \dfrac{1}{4},\ \dfrac{5}{8},\ \dfrac{1}{2}.\)

  1. Make a line plot on a number line marked in eighths.
  2. How many insects are at least \( \dfrac{1}{2} \) inch long?
  3. Find the total length of all insects whose length is at least \( \dfrac{1}{2} \) inch (use addition of fractions).
8. 5.MD.C · Volume of rectangular prisms 12 points
  1. Find each volume:

    (i) rectangular prism, 4 × 5 × 8 cm    (ii) cube, edge 6 cm    (iii) rectangular prism, 7 × 3 × 11 in

  2. Compose / decompose volumes:

    A composite solid is made of two rectangular prisms: the lower 8 × 5 × 4 cm, the upper 4 × 3 × 5 cm sitting on top. Find the total volume.

  3. Find the missing dimension:

    (i) prism volume = 360 cm\(^3\), base area = 40 cm\(^2\), height = ___
    (ii) prism volume = 240 in\(^3\), length 8 in, width 5 in, height = ___
    (iii) cube volume = 216 cm\(^3\), edge = ___

  4. Convert: a tank with volume 12 ft\(^3\) holds how many in\(^3\)? Use 1 ft\(^3\) = 1{,}728 in\(^3\).
9. 5.G.A · Coordinate plane (Quadrant I) 10 points
  1. Plot \(A(2, 5),\ B(7, 5),\ C(7, 2),\ D(2, 2)\) on a coordinate plane. Identify the figure formed and find its perimeter and area.
  2. State the coordinates of the origin. State the coordinates of any point on the \(x\)-axis (other than the origin) and any point on the \(y\)-axis (other than the origin).
  3. Plot \(P(3, 7)\) and \(Q(3, 2)\). Find the distance \(PQ\) by counting units. Then plot \(R(8, 7)\) and find \(PR\).
  4. Generate ordered pairs from a rule. The rule is "\(y\) is twice \(x\), then add 1." Make a table for \(x = 0, 1, 2, 3, 4\). Plot the ordered pairs.
10. 5.G.B · Classify two-dimensional figures 11 points
  1. Classify each polygon by its most specific name:

    (i) 4 sides, 4 right angles, all sides equal    (ii) 4 sides, 2 pairs of parallel sides, no right angles    (iii) 4 sides, exactly 1 pair of parallel sides    (iv) 3 sides, all sides equal    (v) 3 sides, exactly two sides equal    (vi) 3 sides, no sides equal    (vii) a triangle with one 90° angle

  2. Hierarchy: complete each statement with "always," "sometimes," or "never":

    (i) A square is ___ a rectangle.    (ii) A rectangle is ___ a square.    (iii) A parallelogram is ___ a rhombus.    (iv) A trapezoid is ___ a parallelogram (under the exclusive definition).    (v) A rhombus is ___ a parallelogram.    (vi) A square is ___ a rhombus.

  3. Draw a Venn diagram showing the relationships among: quadrilateral, parallelogram, rectangle, rhombus, square, trapezoid.
