Instructions

  • Time limit: 120 minutes. Calculator is not permitted. Standard algorithms (taught in MDE/CCSS Grade 4) should be shown for multi-digit operations.
  • Show all work. Bare answers earn at most half credit.
  • Express fractions in simplest form. Round only when the problem says so.
  • Each problem header cites the CCSS-M code(s) the items target.
1. 4.OA · Multistep word problems, factors, multiples, patterns 10 points
  1. List all factor pairs of each number; classify each as prime or composite.

    (i) 24    (ii) 36    (iii) 17    (iv) 49

  2. Determine whether each is a multiple of 6 (yes/no): 30, 42, 50, 84, 96, 100.
  3. Multistep word problems (write a number sentence, then solve):

    (i) Mr. Patel has 8 boxes of 24 apples each. He gives away 47 apples. How many remain?
    (ii) A bookstore had 248 books. They sold 95 and received a shipment of 132 more. How many books now?
    (iii) A student saves $4 each week for 12 weeks, then spends $19 on a video game. How much remains?

  4. A pattern starts at 4 and follows the rule "add 5." List the first 8 terms. Are all terms even, odd, or do they alternate? Justify in one sentence.
2. 4.NBT.A · Place value 10 points
  1. Write each in word form: 47{,}506; 308{,}042; 2{,}750{,}000.
  2. Write each in expanded form: 9{,}473; 50{,}812; 624{,}107.
  3. State the value of the digit 5 in each: 35{,}492; 587{,}210; 50{,}004; 1{,}256.
  4. Compare each pair using \(<,\ >,\ =\):

    (i) 32{,}458 ___ 32{,}548    (ii) 9{,}999 ___ 10{,}010    (iii) 605{,}004 ___ 605{,}040    (iv) 70{,}000 ___ 7 ten thousands

  5. Round to the place named:

    (i) 4{,}567 to nearest hundred    (ii) 28{,}349 to nearest thousand    (iii) 154{,}998 to nearest ten thousand    (iv) 9{,}450 to nearest hundred

3. 4.NBT.B · Multi-digit addition and subtraction 10 points
  1. Add (standard algorithm; show carries):

    (i) \(4{,}632 + 8{,}547\)    (ii) \(28{,}495 + 17{,}306\)    (iii) \(124{,}580 + 96{,}473\)

  2. Subtract (standard algorithm; show borrowing):

    (i) \(7{,}004 - 2{,}856\)    (ii) \(63{,}500 - 28{,}769\)    (iii) \(200{,}000 - 87{,}453\)

  3. Estimate by rounding to the nearest hundred, then compute exactly: \(478 + 526 - 213\). State the difference between estimate and exact.
4. 4.NBT.B · Multi-digit multiplication 10 points
  1. Compute (single-digit times multi-digit):

    (i) \(243 \times 6\)    (ii) \(1{,}472 \times 8\)    (iii) \(3{,}586 \times 7\)

  2. Compute (two-digit times two-digit; standard algorithm):

    (i) \(54 \times 36\)    (ii) \(78 \times 92\)    (iii) \(45 \times 67\)

  3. Compute (two-digit times three-digit):

    (i) \(238 \times 47\)    (ii) \(412 \times 35\)

  4. Mental math (multiply by multiples of 10/100):

    (i) \(40 \times 600\)    (ii) \(80 \times 90\)    (iii) \(7 \times 4{,}000\)

5. 4.NBT.B · Multi-digit division 10 points
  1. Long division (state quotient and remainder):

    (i) \(486 \div 6\)    (ii) \(875 \div 7\)    (iii) \(2{,}943 \div 4\)    (iv) \(6{,}305 \div 9\)    (v) \(1{,}248 \div 6\) (no remainder check)

  2. Word problem: a school has 736 students arranged in equal groups of 8. How many groups are there?
  3. Word problem: 1{,}245 cookies are placed equally into 5 jars. How many cookies in each jar? Show the long division.
6. 4.NF.A · Fraction equivalence and comparison 10 points
  1. Find an equivalent fraction in simplest form:

    (i) \( \dfrac{6}{8} \)    (ii) \( \dfrac{12}{18} \)    (iii) \( \dfrac{20}{24} \)    (iv) \( \dfrac{15}{45} \)

  2. Write three fractions equivalent to each:

    (i) \( \dfrac{2}{3} \)    (ii) \( \dfrac{3}{5} \)

  3. Compare each pair using \(<,\ >,\ =\). Show your common-denominator work or benchmark reasoning:

    (i) \( \dfrac{2}{3} \) ___ \( \dfrac{3}{5} \)    (ii) \( \dfrac{5}{8} \) ___ \( \dfrac{1}{2} \)    (iii) \( \dfrac{7}{12} \) ___ \( \dfrac{3}{4} \)    (iv) \( \dfrac{4}{9} \) ___ \( \dfrac{2}{5} \)

7. 4.NF.B · Add/subtract fractions, multiply fraction by whole number 10 points
  1. Compute (like denominators); express in simplest form:

    (i) \( \dfrac{2}{6} + \dfrac{3}{6} \)    (ii) \( \dfrac{7}{8} - \dfrac{3}{8} \)    (iii) \( \dfrac{5}{12} + \dfrac{4}{12} - \dfrac{2}{12} \)

  2. Mixed number (regroup if needed):

    (i) \(2\dfrac{1}{5} + 1\dfrac{2}{5}\)    (ii) \(5 - 2\dfrac{3}{4}\)    (iii) \(3\dfrac{2}{6} - 1\dfrac{5}{6}\)

  3. Multiply a fraction by a whole number:

    (i) \(4 \times \dfrac{2}{5}\)    (ii) \(6 \times \dfrac{3}{8}\)    (iii) \(7 \times \dfrac{2}{3}\)

  4. Word problem: 4 students each ate \(\dfrac{3}{8}\) of a pizza. How much pizza did they eat in all? Express as a mixed number.
8. 4.NF.C · Decimal notation for fractions; tenths and hundredths 8 points
  1. Write each fraction as a decimal:

    (i) \( \dfrac{3}{10} \)    (ii) \( \dfrac{47}{100} \)    (iii) \( \dfrac{6}{10} + \dfrac{4}{100} \)    (iv) \( \dfrac{9}{100} \)

  2. Write each decimal as a fraction in simplest form:

    (i) 0.7    (ii) 0.25    (iii) 0.50    (iv) 0.08

  3. Compare each pair:

    (i) 0.7 ___ 0.70    (ii) 0.4 ___ 0.39    (iii) 0.65 ___ \( \dfrac{2}{3} \)

  4. Place each on a number line marked from 0 to 1 in tenths: 0.3, 0.85, \( \dfrac{1}{2} \), \( \dfrac{7}{10} \).
9. 4.MD · Conversions, perimeter, area, angle measurement 12 points
  1. Convert (use 1 ft = 12 in, 1 yd = 3 ft, 1 km = 1000 m, 1 kg = 1000 g, 1 hr = 60 min):

    (i) 5 ft = ___ in    (ii) 4 yd = ___ ft    (iii) 3.5 km = ___ m    (iv) 7{,}200 g = ___ kg    (v) 2 hr 45 min = ___ min

  2. Compute perimeter and area:

    (i) rectangle 14 cm by 9 cm    (ii) square, side 12 in

  3. Find the missing dimension:

    (i) rectangle perimeter 50 m, length 17 m → width ___
    (ii) rectangle area 192 ft\(^2\), width 8 ft → length ___

  4. Angles (use a protractor or by inspection):

    (i) Two angles together form a straight line. One is 47°. Find the other.
    (ii) Two angles together form a right angle. One is 28°. Find the other.
    (iii) An angle is split into two parts of 35° and \(x\)°. The total is 88°. Find \(x\).

  5. Time problem: a movie starts at 2:35 p.m. and lasts 1 hr 47 min. End time?
10. 4.G · Lines, angles, classifying shapes, symmetry 10 points
  1. Identify each as a point, line, line segment, ray, or angle:

    (i) extends forever in two directions    (ii) starts at one endpoint and extends in one direction forever    (iii) two endpoints    (iv) two rays sharing a common endpoint

  2. Classify each angle by measure:

    (i) 30°    (ii) 90°    (iii) 145°    (iv) 180°

  3. Classify each triangle by side lengths and by angles. Sketch each:

    (i) sides 5, 5, 5    (ii) sides 3, 4, 5    (iii) sides 6, 6, 8    (iv) angles 30°, 60°, 90°

  4. Sketch each shape and draw all lines of symmetry. State how many:

    (i) square    (ii) equilateral triangle    (iii) rectangle (non-square)    (iv) regular hexagon    (v) circle
