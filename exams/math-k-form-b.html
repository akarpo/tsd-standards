Kindergarten Mathematics — Standards-Aligned Skills Examination

A calculation- and recognition-focused, standards-walkthrough exam covering the CCSS-M Kindergarten standards (K.CC, K.OA, K.NBT, K.MD, K.G). Each task cites the standard. Form B complements Form A.

Format and proctor instructions

  • Proctor-administered. The proctor reads tasks aloud and writes down what the student says or shows. The student uses real objects (counters, pattern blocks, base-ten blocks).
  • Time: 120 minutes total in three sessions of about 30 minutes, separated by two 10-minute breaks.
  • Materials provided: ~30 same-color counters; pattern blocks (triangle, square, rectangle, hexagon, trapezoid, rhombus); 3-D shape models (cube, cone, cylinder, sphere); base-ten blocks (units and rods); printed numeral cards 0–20; pencil; crayons.
  • Scoring: as listed per task. Award full credit only for correct, quick responses.
  • Each task header cites the CCSS-M code(s) the items target.

SESSION 1 · Tasks 1–3

1. K.CC.A · Counting and numeral writing 14 points
  1. Rote count to 100 by ones. (3 pts: 3 if quick; 2 if at most 2 errors; 1 if reaches 50; 0 otherwise.)

    Errors observed: ____________________     Points: ___ / 3

  2. Count by tens to 100: 10, 20, ___, ___, ___, ___, ___, ___, ___, ___. (2 pts)
  3. Count forward from 47 to 60. (2 pts)
  4. Write each numeral in the box (the proctor calls each):

    0, 3, 6, 9, 12, 15, 18, 20. (2 pts)

  5. Read each numeral aloud (the proctor displays cards): 4, 8, 11, 14, 17, 20. (2 pts)
  6. Name the numeral that comes just after 19 and just before 6. (1 pt)
  7. Write the next number in each sequence: 5, 6, 7, ___; 14, 15, ___; 9, 10, 11, ___. (2 pts)
2. K.CC.B–C · Counting objects and comparing 12 points
  1. Count objects to answer "How many?" The proctor presents:

    (i) 14 stars in a line → ___    (ii) 18 dots in a 3-by-6 array → ___    (iii) 9 fish scattered → ___    (iv) 12 stickers in a circle → ___    (v) 7 toys in a row → ___    (vi) 11 buttons scattered → ___    (4 pts)

  2. Build a group of objects to match a stated quantity. Proctor says: "Show me 8 counters. Now show me 13. Now show me 17." (3 pts)
  3. Compare two groups. The proctor places two piles:

    (i) Pile X = 8 counters, Pile Y = 11 counters. Which has more? Fewer? Difference? (2 pts)
    (ii) Pile A = 6 counters, Pile B = 6 counters. Are they equal? (1 pt)
    (iii) Pile P = 4, Pile Q = 9. By how many is Q larger? (2 pts)

3. K.OA.A · Add within 10 12 points
  1. Find each (counters or fingers; the proctor reads each):

    (i) \(3 + 4\) → ___    (ii) \(5 + 2\) → ___    (iii) \(6 + 4\) → ___    (iv) \(0 + 7\) → ___    (v) \(2 + 5\) → ___    (vi) \(8 + 1\) → ___    (4 pts)

  2. Word problems (proctor reads slowly):

    (i) "There are 4 birds. 3 more come. How many in all?" → ___
    (ii) "There are 5 apples and 2 oranges. How many fruits in all?" → ___
    (iii) "Sam has 6 stickers; his friend gives him 4 more. How many?" → ___ (3 pts)

  3. Decompose 7 into two parts in three different ways. (3 pts)
  4. Make 10. The proctor says: "What number do I add to 6 to make 10? To 4? To 9? To 3?" (4 answers, 0.5 pt each = 2 pts)

— 10-minute break. —

SESSION 2 · Tasks 4–6

4. K.OA.A · Subtract within 10 & quick math facts to 5 12 points
  1. Find each:

    (i) \(8 - 3\) → ___    (ii) \(10 - 6\) → ___    (iii) \(7 - 7\) → ___    (iv) \(9 - 4\) → ___    (v) \(6 - 2\) → ___    (vi) \(10 - 4\) → ___    (4 pts)

  2. Word problems (proctor reads):

    (i) "Mia has 9 cookies. She gives 4 away. How many left?" → ___
    (ii) "10 birds in a tree; 3 fly away. How many remain?" → ___
    (iii) "Anna had 8 stickers; gave some away; now has 5. How many did she give?" → ___ (3 pts)

  3. Fluency within 5 (proctor reads each in quick succession; student answers in under 3 seconds):

    (i) \(5 - 2\)    (ii) \(4 - 1\)    (iii) \(3 + 2\)    (iv) \(5 - 5\)    (v) \(3 + 1\)    (vi) \(5 - 0\)    (vii) \(2 + 2\)    (viii) \(4 - 2\)    (ix) \(1 + 4\)    (x) \(5 - 3\)    (5 pts; 0.5 each)

5. K.NBT.A.1 · Compose and decompose teen numbers 12 points
  1. The proctor places base-ten blocks. The student says how many tens and ones each represents:

    (i) 1 ten + 4 ones = ___    (ii) 1 ten + 7 ones = ___    (iii) 1 ten + 9 ones = ___    (iv) 1 ten + 0 ones = ___    (4 pts)

  2. Break apart each on paper as \(10 + ?\):

    (i) \(13 = 10 + ?\)    (ii) \(15 = 10 + ?\)    (iii) \(18 = 10 + ?\)    (iv) \(11 = 10 + ?\)    (v) \(19 = 10 + ?\)    (5 pts)

  3. Build the number 16 using a 10-frame plus extra counters. Sketch.
  4. Build 12 in two different ways using counters and 10-frames.
6. K.MD · Measurement and classification 10 points
  1. The proctor places three pencils of different lengths. Order them shortest → longest. (2 pts)
  2. The proctor places two objects (book, stuffed animal). Say which is heavier and which is lighter. (2 pts)
  3. Sort buttons into groups by color. The proctor sets out 4 red, 3 blue, 5 yellow. State the count of each.

    red ___, blue ___, yellow ___     Most: ___    Fewest: ___     (3 pts)

  4. Order the three groups from fewest to most. (1 pt)
  5. Use measurement vocabulary in a sentence. The student says: "This pencil is _____ (long / short / heavy / light)." (1 pt)
  6. Compare lengths: which is taller, the door or the desk? Which is wider, the book or the pencil? (1 pt)

— 10-minute break. —

SESSION 3 · Tasks 7–8

7. K.G.A · Identify shapes and position words 14 points
  1. Name each 2-D shape (proctor shows cards): triangle, circle, square, rectangle, hexagon. State the number of sides and corners. (5 pts)
  2. Name each 3-D shape (proctor shows models): cube, cone, cylinder, sphere. Point to one example of each in the room. (4 pts)
  3. Position words. The proctor places objects (book, cup, pencil) on a table.

    (i) "Is the pencil above or below the book?" ___
    (ii) "Is the cup beside or behind the book?" ___
    (iii) "Is the pencil next to or far from the cup?" ___
    (iv) "Place the pencil between the book and the cup."
    (v) "Place the cup in front of the book." (3 pts)

  4. Compare a flat shape and its 3-D counterpart in one phrase each:

    (i) square vs. cube → ___    (ii) circle vs. sphere → ___ (2 pts)

8. K.G.B · Compose simple shapes & final review 14 points
  1. Use pattern blocks. Build a hexagon out of 2 trapezoids; then out of 6 triangles. Check each. (3 pts)
  2. Compose a "house" picture using a square and a triangle. Sketch it. (2 pts)
  3. Build a "house" picture with pattern blocks: a square for the wall and a triangle for the roof. The student names each shape used. (2 pts)
  4. Final mini-review (proctor reads each):

    (i) Count by tens to 50. (1 pt)
    (ii) Find \(7 + 3\). (1 pt)
    (iii) Find \(10 - 2\). (1 pt)
    (iv) Decompose 14 as \(10 + ?\). (1 pt)
    (v) Name the shape with 3 sides. (1 pt)
    (vi) Name the 3-D shape with 6 square faces. (1 pt)
    (vii) Order 4, 9, 2, 6 from least to greatest. (1 pt)
