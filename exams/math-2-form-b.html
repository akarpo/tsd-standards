Instructions

1. 2.OA.B.2 · Add and subtract within 20 (fluency) 15 points
  1. Compute each (recall, no fingers):

    (i) \(8 + 7\)    (ii) \(9 + 6\)    (iii) \(15 - 8\)    (iv) \(13 - 4\)    (v) \(7 + 9\)    (vi) \(17 - 9\)    (vii) \(14 - 6\)    (viii) \(8 + 8\)    (ix) \(6 + 7\)    (x) \(11 - 4\)

  2. Find the missing number:

    (i) \(\square + 6 = 14\)    (ii) \(8 + \square = 17\)    (iii) \(\square - 5 = 9\)    (iv) \(16 - \square = 7\)    (v) \(\square - 4 = 11\)

2. 2.OA.C · Foundations of multiplication (arrays, even/odd) 8 points
  1. Sketch an array showing 3 rows of 5. How many in all? Write a repeated-addition sentence and an early multiplication.
  2. Sketch an array showing 4 rows of 6. How many in all?
  3. Decide whether each is even or odd:

    (i) 13    (ii) 24    (iii) 31    (iv) 50    (v) 47    (vi) 100

  4. Word problem: Mr. Lee has 4 boxes of 8 crayons. How many in all? Write as repeated addition.
3. 2.NBT.A · Place value to 1{,}000 12 points
  1. Write each in expanded form (e.g., \(400 + 20 + 6\)):

    (i) 426    (ii) 308    (iii) 750    (iv) 999

  2. Read each (write in standard form):

    (i) "five hundred eighty-three"    (ii) "two hundred seven"    (iii) "nine hundred forty"    (iv) "six hundred"

  3. Compare each pair using \(<,\ >,\ =\):

    (i) 348 ___ 384    (ii) 600 ___ 599    (iii) 750 ___ \(700 + 50\)    (iv) 9 hundreds ___ 900    (v) 405 ___ 4 hundreds + 5 tens

  4. Skip-count by 5s starting at 105: 105, 110, ___, ___, ___, ___, ___, ___.
  5. Skip-count by 100s starting at 304: 304, ___, ___, ___, ___, ___, ___.
4. 2.NBT.B · Add and subtract within 1{,}000 14 points
  1. Add (standard algorithm; show regrouping):

    (i) \(247 + 158\)    (ii) \(305 + 478\)    (iii) \(384 + 296\)    (iv) \(519 + 273\)

  2. Subtract (standard algorithm; show borrowing):

    (i) \(623 - 246\)    (ii) \(800 - 357\)    (iii) \(740 - 196\)    (iv) \(550 - 273\)

  3. Mental math (show your reasoning):

    (i) \(540 + 60\)    (ii) \(700 - 200\)    (iii) \(390 + 110\)    (iv) \(425 + 25\)    (v) \(480 - 30\)

5. 2.MD.A · Measurement of length 10 points
  1. Use a ruler. Measure each (round to nearest cm and inch):

    (i) a pencil    (ii) a notebook side    (iii) a textbook side

  2. Add lengths: a 14-cm yarn and a 23-cm yarn laid end-to-end.
  3. Subtract lengths: a 65-in board after 28 in is sawed off.
  4. Compare: a 25-cm pencil and a 7-in pencil. Which is longer? (1 in ≈ 2.54 cm; you may estimate.)
  5. Plot 14, 18, 22, 30 on a number line marked from 0 to 40.
6. 2.MD.C · Time and money 12 points
  1. Read each clock to the nearest 5 minutes:

    (i) hour hand near 3, minute hand on 6 → ___:___    (ii) hour hand near 7, minute hand on 4 → ___:___    (iii) hour hand near 11, minute hand on 9 → ___:___

  2. Time arithmetic:

    (i) It is 4:15 p.m. What time will it be in 30 minutes? In 1 hr 15 min?
    (ii) A class begins at 10:25 a.m. and lasts 45 minutes. End time?

  3. Count each set of coins (use $ and ¢ symbols correctly):

    (i) 3 quarters, 2 dimes, 1 nickel
    (ii) 1 dollar bill, 4 quarters, 3 pennies
    (iii) 2 dollar bills, 5 dimes, 6 nickels
    (iv) 4 quarters, 7 dimes, 8 nickels, 12 pennies

  4. A student has $5. They buy a book for $2.75 and a pencil for $0.85. How much money do they have left? Show the subtraction with dollars and cents.
7. 2.MD.D · Bar and picture graphs 10 points

Class survey of favorite recess: Tag 10, Soccer 8, Swings 6, Books 4.

  1. How many students were surveyed in all?
  2. How many more chose Tag than Books?
  3. If two students changed from Soccer to Books, what would the new totals be? Make a small table.
  4. Make a bar graph with y-axis marked in 2s. Label both axes and give a title.
  5. Make a picture graph with key "smile = 2 students."
8. 2.G · Shapes and partitioning 11 points
  1. Identify each shape and state how many sides and how many angles it has:

    (i) triangle    (ii) quadrilateral    (iii) pentagon    (iv) hexagon

  2. Partition a rectangle into 2 equal parts (halves) in two different ways. Sketch each.
  3. Partition a circle into 4 equal parts (fourths). Sketch and shade one fourth.
  4. Partition a rectangle into 3 equal parts (thirds). Sketch.
  5. Partition a square into 6 equal parts. Sketch one possible way.
  6. Identify the 3-D shape with 6 square faces, 8 vertices, 12 edges. Sketch it.
  7. Identify the 3-D shape with 1 circular base and 1 vertex (apex). Sketch it.
9. 2.OA.A · Word problems 8 points
  1. Maya had 47 marbles. She gave 18 to her sister, then her grandmother gave her 25 more. How many now? (Two steps; number sentences.)
  2. A library has 234 fiction and 168 non-fiction books. Total? If 75 are checked out, how many remain on the shelves?
  3. A baker made 56 cookies on Monday and 73 on Tuesday. He sold 89. How many remain?
  4. Mrs. Park collected $5 from each of 8 students for a trip. After paying $24 for snacks, how much was left?
